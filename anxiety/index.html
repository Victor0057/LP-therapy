About your care

When anxiety gets loud, it feels like your body is the enemy.

Anxiety tries to protect you using fear. It creates false alarms and your body reacts like there is danger. Tight chest, dizziness, nausea, racing heart, short breath, or the sudden dread that something terrible is about to happen. That is what panic attacks can feel like.

CBT retrains the alarm system. You learn to recognize the pattern, reduce safety habits that keep the cycle alive, and practice skills that lower symptoms over time. The goal is not to force yourself to be fearless. The goal is to feel safe inside your own body again.

Result you can expect: calmer mornings, fewer spirals, more confidence in conversations, better sleep, and the ability to go places without scanning for danger.

FAQ

Questions people ask before they start.

What if my symptoms feel extreme, like I am losing control?+

Panic symptoms can feel dangerous, but they are often a false alarm response. In CBT we work on two things: reducing fear of sensations and reducing the habits that keep the alarm active. As fear drops, symptoms usually lose intensity and duration.

How soon do people feel a difference?+

Many people notice early changes when they learn the pattern and stop feeding it. The deeper change comes from repetition: using skills during real moments and gradually returning to avoided places. We track progress in what you can actually do, not in perfect feelings.

Do I need to talk about everything from my past?+

Not necessarily. We focus on what is keeping the anxiety cycle alive today. If past experiences matter for your current triggers, we address them in a way that supports your goals and stability.

Is online therapy effective for anxiety and panic attacks?+

For many people, yes. The work is skills and real life practice, and you can do that from home. Online sessions can also be easier to start because you are in your safe space.

Will this be confidential?+

Yes. Sessions are private and designed to protect your confidentiality. If you have specific questions about privacy for your region or provider, ask before you start.
